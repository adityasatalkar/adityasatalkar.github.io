How I got into the field of Computer Science and what all I've done so far

When I was first introduced to a computer I found myself intrigued by it. It was only when I turned twelve, I attended an introductory session at school on Microsoft’s ‘Frontpage’, a tool to create websites, which was all it took to set the cogs in motion. So, I created my first website on ‘India’ , which had troves of information on the cultural diversity of India. It garnered over a thousand hits within the first month. It was at this golden moment, I vowed to myself that I needed to be an integral part of the information revolution sweeping the world. I set myself on a path that would lead me to the world of computers. After my 10th grade, I enrolled for a three year diploma in Computer Science. I then went on to obtain my Bachelor of Engineering degree in Information Technology at the University of Pune. And I am currently pursuing Masters in Computer Science at New Jersey Institute of Technology.

My first job was as a Software Developer Intern at Persistent Systems Limited.
 

In 2013, a person from Persistent Systems visited our college and asked our Head of the Department a list of students who she thought excelled in class. Obviously my name was not in the list and I thought this was not fair. So I wrote an email to one of the HRs at Persistent Systems saying even I'd like an opportunity to work with the company and so the folks at Persistent Systems gladly invited to test our skill sets. Me along with my team mates were able to crack the test and were offered an year long project.

There were four of us collaborating in different capacities, and at no point was anyone territorial.

So I contacted the team and spoke with his manager at persistent Systems and started working on the project that was assigned to us. The project was to Develop a Platform Independent Test Automation Framework using Selenium, Java and TestNG."There were four of us collaborating in different capacities, and at no point was anyone territorial." What started as a just an email side project quickly became our full-time jobs later on as we were working day and night during the last six months of the college just on the project.

The main function of the software was to automate the test cases using the input values given to the software in the form of an XML file or Excel data sheets.

The software then automated the tests that were to be carried out from the test cases specified. This project helped the company save about $7000 in license costs.

After my project at Persistent Systems was done I went to Smart Learning Pvt Ltd
 

So, what do you do just after having finished working for one of the biggest MNCs in India. Well thats a tough question because the way these companies work is totally different from how any other software company might work, because the scale is very different. So I now wanted to work for a start-up and gain some more experience.

Luckily I knew just the right place to look for. My father's friend had just incubated this Software Start-up which is an E-Learning software company Smart Learning Pvt Ltd. So I started working with them on this great project where they were integrating the software with these cool animated videos that they made in-house for the software. I played a leadership role in the integration part of those videos with the software module.

In a start-up its all about the company and the team work and how you get it done quickly without there being any delay in the process.

I Then joined The Magnum Technologies
 

After this work was done I then took a few months off as I went back to school. I started with my master's program. Then I went back to India during the Summer of 2015 as I was not allowed to do an internship in the US due a few rules imposed on Spring admit students. But since I was in India I got in touch with my college friend who has started his own software company Magnum Technologies and had been floating it successfully since two years. So I started working with him at his office as an Associate Web Apps Developer. The team only consisted of a total of eight members back then, but it was great !

"I remember the first time our director and manager were testing it out and editing the same doc at the same time. They started making fun of each other in the doc, deleting each other's edits, writing jokes and insults and I'm there sweating bullets worried that something would break!"

It didn't break, and the real-time collaboration engine the team built was generalized and now powers similar functionality not only in Docs, but in Google's entire Apps suite as well as countless apps from third-party developers using the Google Drive Realtime API.
